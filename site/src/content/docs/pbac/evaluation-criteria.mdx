# PBAC Evaluation Criteria

PBAC evaluates every submission against multiple criteria to determine whether a medicine provides sufficient value to justify PBS subsidy. No single criterion is decisive — PBAC weighs all factors together.

## The Six Criteria

### 1. Clinical Effectiveness

PBAC assesses whether the new medicine works better than the **comparator** — the treatment it would replace or supplement in clinical practice.

- Evidence comes from randomised controlled trials and systematic reviews
- PBAC looks at both efficacy (trial results) and likely effectiveness (real-world impact)
- The comparator is the most common treatment currently used for the same condition in Australia

<Aside type="note">
Choosing the right comparator is critical. If the sponsor proposes an inappropriate comparator, PBAC may reject the submission on that basis alone.
</Aside>

### 2. Cost-Effectiveness

This is often the most influential criterion. PBAC calculates the **incremental cost-effectiveness ratio (ICER):**

```
ICER = (Cost of new drug - Cost of comparator) / (Health benefit of new - Health benefit of comparator)
```

The result is expressed as **cost per QALY gained** (Quality-Adjusted Life Year).

- PBAC does not have an official willingness-to-pay threshold
- In practice, submissions with an ICER under $45,000 per QALY are generally considered acceptable
- ICERs between $45,000 and $75,000 per QALY may be acceptable with strong justification
- ICERs above $75,000 per QALY face significant scrutiny

<Aside type="tip">
**Agent tip:** ICER values and cost-effectiveness assessments are not available in the PBS API. They are published in PBAC Public Summary Documents on [pbac.pbs.gov.au](https://pbac.pbs.gov.au). The API contains the **outcome** of these evaluations (i.e., the listed price and restrictions) but not the evaluation inputs.
</Aside>

### 3. Budget Impact

PBAC estimates the total cost to the PBS over **5 years** if the medicine is listed. This includes:

- Estimated number of patients who will use the medicine
- Cost per patient per year
- Offset savings from reduced use of the comparator
- Market growth assumptions

Medicines with high budget impact (tens of millions per year) receive extra scrutiny, even if cost-effective per patient.

### 4. Patient Impact

PBAC considers the clinical significance for patients:

- **Severity** of the condition being treated
- **Unmet need** — are there existing treatments available?
- **Quality of life** improvement expected
- **Life expectancy** impact

### 5. Equity and Rule of Rescue

PBAC applies the **Rule of Rescue** in cases where:

- The condition is life-threatening or causes very severe illness
- There are no alternative treatments available
- The number of affected patients is small
- The medicine provides a meaningful clinical benefit

Under Rule of Rescue, PBAC may accept a higher ICER than it normally would.

### 6. Uncertainty

PBAC assesses confidence in the evidence:

- **Quality of trials** — well-designed RCTs vs observational data
- **Relevance to Australian practice** — are trial populations representative?
- **Extrapolation** — are long-term benefits being projected from short-term data?
- **Model assumptions** — how sensitive are cost-effectiveness results to key assumptions?

High uncertainty often leads to **deferred** decisions or **managed-entry schemes** where listing is conditional on further data collection.

## How Criteria Map to API Data

While the evaluation criteria themselves are not in the PBS API, their outcomes are visible:

| Criterion Outcome | Where It Appears in the API |
|-------------------|-----------------------------|
| Clinical restrictions from evaluation | [`restrictions`](/endpoints/restrictions/) and [`criteria`](/endpoints/criteria/) |
| Agreed price after cost-effectiveness review | [`fees`](/endpoints/fees/) endpoint |
| Budget impact conditions | [`dispensing-rules`](/endpoints/dispensing-rules/) (quantity limits, repeats) |
| Authority requirements from uncertainty | [`restrictions`](/endpoints/restrictions/) authority method field |
