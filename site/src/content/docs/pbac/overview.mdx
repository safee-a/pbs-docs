# PBAC Overview

The **Pharmaceutical Benefits Advisory Committee (PBAC)** is the independent statutory body that decides which medicines should be subsidized on the PBS. Its recommendations are the gateway to PBS listing.

## What PBAC Is

- **Statutory committee** established under Section 101 of the National Health Act 1953
- **Independent from government** — the Health Minister cannot override a negative PBAC recommendation
- Makes **recommendations** to the Minister on which medicines should be listed
- All decisions are published as **Public Summary Documents (PSDs)**
- Website: [pbac.pbs.gov.au](https://pbac.pbs.gov.au)

<Aside type="note">
PBAC's independence is a key feature of Australia's PBS system. Unlike many other countries, the government cannot unilaterally decide to subsidize a medicine — it must first receive a positive recommendation from PBAC.
</Aside>

## Committee Composition

PBAC has approximately **18 members** appointed by the Health Minister, including:

| Member Type | Role |
|-------------|------|
| Clinicians | Medical expertise across specialties |
| Health economists | Cost-effectiveness assessment |
| Pharmacists | Dispensing and pharmaceutical expertise |
| Consumer representatives | Patient and public perspective |
| Epidemiologists | Evidence evaluation |

The committee chair is typically a senior clinician. Members serve fixed terms and must declare conflicts of interest.

## What PBAC Evaluates

For each submission, PBAC considers:

1. **Clinical effectiveness** — does the medicine work better than existing treatments?
2. **Cost-effectiveness** — is the health benefit worth the cost compared to alternatives?
3. **Budget impact** — what will this cost the government over 5 years?
4. **Patient need** — how severe is the condition, and are alternatives available?
5. **Equity considerations** — including the "Rule of Rescue" for life-threatening conditions

See [evaluation criteria](/pbac/evaluation-criteria/) for a detailed breakdown of how PBAC weighs these factors.

## Meeting Schedule

PBAC meets **three times per year**:

| Meeting | Submission Deadline |
|---------|-------------------|
| March | ~November (prior year) |
| July | ~March |
| November | ~July |

Submissions must be lodged approximately 4 months before the meeting. See [timeline and cycles](/listing-process/timeline-and-cycles/) for detailed scheduling.

## Decision Outcomes

After evaluation, PBAC makes one of four decisions:

- **Recommended** — positive recommendation, proceeds to pricing
- **Recommended with restrictions** — listed for specific patient groups only
- **Deferred** — more information requested from sponsor
- **Not recommended** — rejected, sponsor can resubmit with new evidence

See [outcomes and recommendations](/pbac/outcomes-and-recommendations/) for details on each outcome type.

## Public Summary Documents (PSDs)

PBAC publishes a **Public Summary Document** for every decision, including rejections. PSDs contain:

- The clinical question addressed
- Summary of evidence considered
- PBAC's assessment of clinical and cost-effectiveness
- The committee's recommendation and reasoning

<Aside type="tip">
**Agent tip:** PSDs are not available through the PBS API. They are published on [pbac.pbs.gov.au](https://pbac.pbs.gov.au). To connect PBAC decisions to API data, match the medicine's drug name or PBS code from the PSD to the [`items`](/endpoints/items/) endpoint.
</Aside>

## PBAC vs Other Committees

| Committee | Evaluates | Feeds Into |
|-----------|-----------|------------|
| **PBAC** | Medicines for PBS subsidy | PBS schedule |
| **MSAC** | Medical services and procedures | Medicare Benefits Schedule |
| **ATAGI** | Vaccine recommendations | National Immunisation Program |

Only PBAC decisions are reflected in the PBS API data.
