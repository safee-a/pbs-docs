# PBAC Outcomes and Recommendations

After evaluating a submission, PBAC issues one of four outcomes. Each outcome has different implications for the sponsor and for what eventually appears in the PBS API.

## Outcome Types

### Recommended

A straightforward positive recommendation. PBAC is satisfied that the medicine is clinically effective and cost-effective. The submission proceeds to:

1. **PBPA** for price negotiation
2. **Minister** for final approval
3. **PBS schedule** listing on the next available monthly update

### Recommended with Restrictions

PBAC recommends listing, but only for **specific patient groups or conditions**. This is the most common positive outcome.

Restrictions may specify:
- The clinical indication (e.g., "for treatment of moderate to severe rheumatoid arthritis")
- Prior treatment requirements (e.g., "patient must have tried and failed first-line therapy")
- Prescriber limitations (e.g., "must be prescribed by a specialist")
- Authority method (streamlined, telephone, written, or electronic)

<Aside type="note">
Most PBS items have restrictions. Unrestricted listings are the exception, not the rule. The restrictions that PBAC recommends become the prescribing rules published in the PBS schedule.
</Aside>

### Deferred

PBAC has concerns but is not ready to reject the submission outright. Common reasons for deferral:

- Insufficient clinical evidence — PBAC wants additional data
- Uncertainty in the economic model — key assumptions need validation
- Need for a risk-sharing arrangement — PBAC wants pricing conditions
- Comparator disagreement — PBAC and sponsor disagree on the appropriate comparator

The sponsor addresses PBAC's concerns and the submission is reconsidered at the **next meeting** (4-8 months later).

### Not Recommended (Rejected)

PBAC concludes the medicine does not provide acceptable value. Common reasons:

- Clinical benefit is not demonstrated vs the comparator
- ICER is too high and cannot be justified
- Uncertainty in evidence is too great
- Budget impact is unacceptable relative to the benefit

The sponsor can prepare a **resubmission** with new evidence, a revised economic model, or a lower price. See [submission types](/pbac/submission-types/) for resubmission details.

## From Recommendation to API Data

After a positive PBAC recommendation, the following sequence occurs:

```
PBAC Recommendation
       ↓
PBPA Price Negotiation (1-4 months)
       ↓
Minister Approval (1-2 months)
       ↓
PBS Schedule Update (1st of month)
       ↓
Data available in API
```

<Aside type="tip">
**Agent tip:** New listings appear in the API on the 1st of each month. Use the [`summary-of-changes`](/endpoints/summary-of-changes/) endpoint to detect them. New items will have a `schedule_code` matching the month they were added.
</Aside>

## How Restrictions Map to API Data

PBAC's recommended restrictions translate directly into the API's restriction hierarchy:

| PBAC Recommendation | API Representation |
|--------------------|--------------------|
| Clinical indication text | `RESTRICTION_TEXT` in [`restrictions`](/endpoints/restrictions/) |
| Condition groups | Parameters in [`parameters`](/endpoints/parameters/) |
| Specific clinical requirements | Criteria in [`criteria`](/endpoints/criteria/) |
| Authority method (streamlined, phone, written) | `AUTHORITY_METHOD` in [`restrictions`](/endpoints/restrictions/) |
| Prescriber type limitations | `PRESCRIBER_CODE` linking to [`prescribers`](/endpoints/prescribers/) |

For a complete picture of an item's restrictions, use the [`item-overview`](/endpoints/item-overview/) endpoint which returns the full restriction hierarchy in a single response.

See [Restriction System](/concepts/restriction-system/) for details on navigating the three-level hierarchy.

## Public Summary Documents

PBAC publishes a **Public Summary Document (PSD)** for every decision, including rejections. PSDs are the primary public record of PBAC's reasoning and contain:

- Summary of the submission
- Clinical evidence reviewed
- Economic evaluation results
- PBAC's deliberation and reasoning
- The final recommendation

PSDs are available on [pbac.pbs.gov.au](https://pbac.pbs.gov.au) and are published after each meeting round. They are not available through the PBS API.

## Tracking Outcomes Over Time

To monitor how PBAC decisions flow into the PBS:

1. Check PBAC outcome publications after each meeting (March, July, November)
2. Watch for new items in the API 2-4 months after positive recommendations
3. Compare [`items`](/endpoints/items/) across consecutive schedule codes to spot new listings
4. Use [`summary-of-changes`](/endpoints/summary-of-changes/) for a structured view of additions and modifications
