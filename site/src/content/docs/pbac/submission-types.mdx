# PBAC Submission Types

Sponsors submit different types of applications to PBAC depending on whether the medicine is new, already listed, or being resubmitted after a previous decision.

## Submission Categories

| Type | When Used | Evidence Required | Typical Timeline |
|------|-----------|-------------------|-----------------|
| **Major** | New medicine or new indication | Full clinical + economic evaluation | 9-17 months |
| **Minor** | Changes to existing listing | Abbreviated assessment | 6-9 months |
| **Resubmission** | After rejection or deferral | New or updated evidence | Additional 4-12 months |
| **Section 100** | Section 100 program listings | Program-specific requirements | Varies |
| **Price increase** | Request to raise listed price | Strong justification required | 6-12 months |

## Major Submissions

A major submission is required when a sponsor seeks to list a **new medicine** or a **new therapeutic indication** for an already-listed medicine.

Major submissions must include:

- **Clinical evidence** — systematic review of clinical trials comparing the medicine to the appropriate comparator
- **Economic evaluation** — cost-effectiveness analysis (cost-utility or cost-effectiveness model)
- **Budget impact analysis** — estimated cost to the PBS over 5 years
- **Proposed restriction wording** — the clinical criteria for prescribing access

<Aside type="note">
Major submissions are the most common pathway for new medicines entering the PBS. They receive the most rigorous evaluation from PBAC.
</Aside>

## Minor Submissions

Minor submissions cover changes to **existing PBS listings** that do not require a full clinical evaluation:

- New dosage form or strength of an already-listed medicine
- Changes to restriction wording (broadening or narrowing access)
- Changes to maximum quantities or repeats
- Voluntary price reductions

These receive a **streamlined assessment** because the clinical evidence base has already been evaluated.

## Resubmissions

When PBAC **defers or rejects** a submission, the sponsor can resubmit. Resubmissions must address the specific concerns raised by PBAC:

- New clinical trial data or longer-term follow-up results
- Revised economic model addressing PBAC's uncertainty concerns
- Lower proposed price to improve cost-effectiveness
- Revised restriction wording to target a more appropriate patient population

<Aside type="tip">
**Agent tip:** When a previously rejected medicine appears in the [`items`](/endpoints/items/) endpoint for the first time, it means a resubmission was eventually successful. Check the PBAC Public Summary Document on [pbac.pbs.gov.au](https://pbac.pbs.gov.au) for the evaluation history.
</Aside>

## Section 100 Submissions

Section 100 of the National Health Act covers special supply arrangements. Submissions for Section 100 programs follow a modified process tailored to each program's requirements.

Common Section 100 programs include:
- Highly Specialised Drugs (HSD)
- Efficient Funding of Chemotherapy (EFC)
- Remote Area Aboriginal Health Services

See [PBS Programs](/concepts/pbs-programs/) for details on each Section 100 scheme and how they appear in the API.

## Price Increase Applications

Price increase submissions are **rare** and require strong justification. PBAC will only consider increases when:

- Manufacturing costs have genuinely increased
- Supply continuity is at risk without a price adjustment
- The medicine remains cost-effective at the higher price

Most PBS price movements are **downward** due to statutory price reductions, price disclosure, and generic competition.

## How Submission Type Affects API Data

The submission type is not directly visible in the PBS API. However, the outcomes are reflected in different data patterns:

| Submission Type | What Changes in the API |
|-----------------|------------------------|
| Major (new medicine) | New `pbs_code` appears in [`items`](/endpoints/items/) |
| Major (new indication) | New [`restrictions`](/endpoints/restrictions/) added to existing item |
| Minor (form/strength) | New item entry with same `drug_name`, different `li_form` |
| Minor (restriction change) | Updated restriction text in [`restrictions`](/endpoints/restrictions/) |
| Price increase | Higher price values in [`fees`](/endpoints/fees/) |
