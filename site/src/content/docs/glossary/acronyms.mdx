# PBS Acronyms

Quick reference table for acronyms found in PBS documentation, API responses, and related healthcare systems.

<Aside type="tip">
**Agent tip:** Use this table for fast acronym resolution. For full definitions and context, see the [PBS glossary](/glossary/terms/).
</Aside>

| Acronym | Full Name | Description |
|---------|-----------|-------------|
| AEMP | Approved Ex-Manufacturer Price | Government-approved price the manufacturer charges before markups |
| AMT | Australian Medicines Terminology | SNOMED CT-AU based terminology for identifying medicines. See [AMT integration](/data-integration/amt-integration/) |
| ATC | Anatomical Therapeutic Chemical | WHO classification system that groups medicines by organ system and therapeutic purpose |
| CTPP | Containered Trade Product Pack | AMT concept — the most specific level, identifying the exact packaged product |
| DPMQ | Dispensed Price for Maximum Quantity | Total price as dispensed to the patient, including markup and fees |
| DVA | Department of Veterans' Affairs | Administers the Repatriation PBS (RPBS) for eligible veterans |
| EFC | Efficient Funding of Chemotherapy | Section 100 program for hospital-administered chemotherapy medicines |
| HPOS | Health Professional Online Services | Online portal where prescribers submit authority requests to Services Australia |
| HSD | Highly Specialised Drugs | Section 100 program for medicines dispensed through hospital pharmacies |
| ICER | Incremental Cost-Effectiveness Ratio | Cost per QALY gained; the primary metric PBAC uses to assess value for money |
| LEMI | Lowest Expected Market Indicator | Reference price benchmark derived from price disclosure data |
| MP | Medicinal Product | AMT concept — generic substance level (e.g., paracetamol) |
| MPCPP | Maximum Premium Claimable by Premium-Free Pharmacists | Markup limit for certain pharmacy categories |
| MPP | Medicinal Product Pack | AMT concept — generic medicine at pack level (e.g., paracetamol 500 mg tablet, 20) |
| MPUU | Medicinal Product Unit of Use | AMT concept — generic medicine at unit level (e.g., paracetamol 500 mg tablet) |
| MSAC | Medical Services Advisory Committee | Evaluates medical devices and procedures (parallel body to PBAC) |
| PBAC | Pharmaceutical Benefits Advisory Committee | Independent body that evaluates and recommends medicines for PBS listing |
| PBPA | Pharmaceutical Benefits Pricing Authority | Negotiates PBS medicine prices with pharmaceutical companies |
| PBS | Pharmaceutical Benefits Scheme | Australia's national medicine subsidy program under the National Health Act 1953 |
| PSD | Public Summary Document | Published rationale for PBAC's recommendation on each medicine listing |
| QALY | Quality-Adjusted Life Year | Health outcome measure combining survival duration and quality of life |
| RPBS | Repatriation Pharmaceutical Benefits Scheme | PBS program for veterans and war widows, administered by DVA |
| TGA | Therapeutic Goods Administration | Australian regulatory authority for medicine safety, quality, and efficacy |
| TP | Trade Product | AMT concept — branded medicine name (e.g., Panadol) |
| TPP | Trade Product Pack | AMT concept — branded medicine at pack level (e.g., Panadol 500 mg tablet, 20) |
| TPUU | Trade Product Unit of Use | AMT concept — branded medicine at unit level (e.g., Panadol 500 mg tablet) |
| WADP | Weighted Average Disclosed Price | Average real transaction price reported by manufacturers under the price disclosure policy |

## AMT Concept Hierarchy

The AMT acronyms above form a hierarchy from generic to specific:

```
MP → MPUU → MPP        (generic path)
TP → TPUU → TPP → CTPP (branded path)
```

For more detail on how AMT concepts relate to PBS data, see [AMT integration](/data-integration/amt-integration/).
