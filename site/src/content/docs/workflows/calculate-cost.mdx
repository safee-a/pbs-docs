# Calculate What a Patient Pays

Determine how much a patient pays out-of-pocket for a PBS prescription.

## Goal

Given a PBS item and patient category (general or concessional), calculate the patient's copayment amount.

## Background

Under the PBS, patients pay a **copayment** that depends on:
1. **Patient category** — General or Concessional (pensioners, healthcare card holders)
2. **Safety net status** — Whether the patient has reached the annual Safety Net threshold
3. **Medicine price** — The dispensed price of the specific item

### 1. Get Current Copayment Amounts

**Endpoint:** [`copayments`](/endpoints/copayments/)

```bash
curl "https://data-api.health.gov.au/pbs/api/v3/copayments?schedule_code=2026-02-01"
```

This returns the current copayment amounts and safety net thresholds for each patient category.

### 2. Get the Item's Pricing

**Endpoint:** [`items`](/endpoints/items/) or [`item-overview`](/endpoints/item-overview/)

```bash
curl "https://data-api.health.gov.au/pbs/api/v3/item-overview?schedule_code=2026-02-01&pbs_code=2621B"
```

Key pricing fields:
- `DISPENSED_PRICE` — total price as dispensed
- `MANUFACTURER_EX_PRICE` — ex-manufacturer price

### 3. Get Fee Components

**Endpoint:** [`fees`](/endpoints/fees/)

```bash
curl "https://data-api.health.gov.au/pbs/api/v3/fees?schedule_code=2026-02-01&pbs_code=2621B"
```

### 4. Calculate the Copayment

The patient pays the **lesser** of:
- The applicable copayment amount (from step 1), OR
- The dispensed price (if lower than the copayment)

## Copayment Categories

| Category | Description | Typical copayment |
|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| **General** | Standard PBS patients | ~$31 (changes annually) |
| **Concessional** | Pensioners, healthcare card holders | ~$7.70 (changes annually) |
| **General Safety Net** | After threshold reached | Reduced rate |
| **Concessional Safety Net** | After threshold reached | Free |

<Aside type="note">
Exact copayment amounts change with each schedule. Always fetch current values from the `copayments` endpoint rather than hardcoding.
</Aside>

## Complete Example

What does a general patient pay for atorvastatin 40mg?

```bash
# 1. Get copayment amounts
curl ".../copayments?schedule_code=2026-02-01"
# → General copayment: $31.60 (example)

# 2. Get item pricing
curl ".../item-overview?schedule_code=2026-02-01&pbs_code=8747L"
# → DISPENSED_PRICE: $15.50 (example)

# 3. Patient pays: min($31.60, $15.50) = $15.50
# (Dispensed price is less than copayment, so patient pays the full dispensed price)
```

## Common Pitfalls

- **Don't hardcode copayments** — They change with each schedule. Always fetch current values.
- **Below-copayment items** — If the dispensed price is below the copayment, the patient pays the full dispensed price (no government subsidy needed).
- **Brand premiums** — If a patient requests a specific brand when cheaper generics exist, they may pay a brand premium on top of the copayment.
- **Safety net tracking** — The API doesn't track individual patient safety net status. Your application needs to track prescriptions dispensed to determine if the threshold has been reached.
