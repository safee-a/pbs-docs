# Copayment Structure

PBS patients contribute a capped copayment towards the cost of their prescriptions. The copayment amount depends on the patient's category and whether they have reached the annual Safety Net threshold.

## Patient Categories

There are two main patient categories:

| Category | Who Qualifies | Standard Copayment | Safety Net Copayment | Annual Threshold |
|----------|--------------|-------------------|---------------------|-----------------|
| **General** | All Medicare-eligible patients | ~$31.60 | Reduced rate | ~$1,563.50 |
| **Concessional** | Pensioners, healthcare card holders | ~$7.70 | Free ($0.00) | ~$262.00 |

<Aside type="note">
Copayment amounts and Safety Net thresholds are indexed annually on 1 January. The figures above are approximate. Always fetch current values from the [`copayments`](/endpoints/copayments/) endpoint rather than hardcoding.
</Aside>

## How Copayments Work

The patient pays the **lesser** of:
- The applicable copayment amount for their category, OR
- The full dispensed price (if the dispensed price is below the copayment)

For example, if a medicine's dispensed price is $12.00 and the general copayment is $31.60, the patient pays just $12.00 — the government does not subsidize the difference.

## Safety Net

After a patient (or family) accumulates enough copayments in a calendar year, they reach the **Safety Net threshold**:

### General Safety Net
- Triggered after the patient's household reaches ~$1,563.50 in copayments
- Once reached, the copayment drops to a reduced rate for the remainder of the year
- The patient receives a Safety Net Concession Card

### Concessional Safety Net
- Triggered after ~$262.00 in copayments (approximately 34 prescriptions)
- Once reached, all PBS prescriptions are **free** for the remainder of the year
- The patient receives a Safety Net Entitlement Card

<Aside type="tip">
**Agent tip:** The PBS API does not track individual patient Safety Net status. Your application must track copayments per patient to determine if thresholds have been reached. The [`copayments`](/endpoints/copayments/) endpoint provides the current threshold amounts.
</Aside>

### Safety Net Reset

Safety Net thresholds **reset on 1 January** each year. Patients must re-accumulate copayments from zero in the new year.

## Brand Premiums

When a patient chooses a **brand-name medicine** and a cheaper generic (bioequivalent) alternative exists:

- The patient pays the standard copayment **plus** a brand premium
- The brand premium is the difference between the chosen brand's price and the benchmark price
- Pharmacists must inform patients when a cheaper alternative is available
- If the patient accepts the generic, no brand premium applies

## Below-Copayment Items

Some medicines have a dispensed price below the general copayment. For these items:
- **General patients** pay the full dispensed price (no government subsidy)
- **Concessional patients** still pay only the concessional copayment (or the dispensed price if even lower)
- These items still count towards the Safety Net threshold

```bash
# Get current copayment amounts and thresholds
curl "https://data-api.health.gov.au/pbs/api/v3/copayments?schedule_code=2026-02-01"
```
