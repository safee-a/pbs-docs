# Price Types

The PBS uses several distinct price measures throughout the supply chain. Understanding which price applies at each stage is essential for correctly interpreting API data.

## Price Type Reference

| Price Type | What It Represents | Found In API |
|------------|-------------------|--------------|
| **Ex-manufacturer price** | Price the manufacturer charges wholesalers | `items` — `manufacturer_ex_price` field |
| **AEMP** | Government-approved ex-manufacturer price | `items` — pricing fields |
| **DPMQ** | Total dispensed price for a standard pack | `items` — `dispensed_price` field |
| **Dispensing fee** | Fee paid to pharmacist for dispensing | `fees` endpoint |
| **Wholesale markup** | Distribution margin | Calculated from `markup-bands` |
| **Pharmacy markup** | Retail margin | Calculated from `markup-bands` |

## Ex-Manufacturer Price

The price the manufacturer charges the wholesaler, before any markups or fees are added. This is the starting point for PBS price calculations.

## Approved Ex-Manufacturer Price (AEMP)

The **AEMP** is the government-approved ex-manufacturer price. It may differ from the actual price charged to wholesalers. The AEMP is what the PBS uses as the basis for calculating the dispensed price.

<Aside type="note">
The AEMP and the actual ex-manufacturer price can diverge, especially after price disclosure cycles reduce the PBS-listed price below what some manufacturers charge.
</Aside>

## DPMQ — Dispensed Price for Maximum Quantity

The total price for a standard pack as dispensed to the patient. DPMQ includes:
- Ex-manufacturer price (AEMP)
- Wholesale markup
- Pharmacy markup
- Dispensing fee
- Container fee

This is the price that determines whether the patient pays a copayment or the full price.

## Reference Pricing

Medicines in the same **therapeutic group** (interchangeable for clinical purposes) are priced relative to the cheapest option in the group. The PBS will subsidize up to the price of the benchmark (lowest-priced) medicine.

- If a patient chooses a medicine priced above the benchmark, they pay the **therapeutic group premium** on top of their copayment
- This encourages use of lower-cost alternatives within the same therapeutic group

## Therapeutic Group Premiums

When multiple brands or formulations exist in a therapeutic group:

- The PBS sets a **benchmark price** based on the lowest-cost option
- Brands priced above the benchmark attract a **premium**
- The patient pays: copayment + therapeutic group premium (if applicable)

<Aside type="tip">
**Agent tip:** To check if a medicine has a therapeutic group premium, compare its dispensed price against other items with the same drug name or ATC code. The [`items`](/endpoints/items/) endpoint includes pricing fields, and [`item-overview`](/endpoints/item-overview/) provides a consolidated view.
</Aside>

## Querying Prices via the API

```bash
# Get item pricing including dispensed price
curl "https://data-api.health.gov.au/pbs/api/v3/items?schedule_code=2026-02-01&pbs_code=2621B"

# Get fee breakdown for an item
curl "https://data-api.health.gov.au/pbs/api/v3/fees?schedule_code=2026-02-01&pbs_code=2621B"
```
