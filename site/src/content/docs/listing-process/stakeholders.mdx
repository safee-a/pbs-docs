# Stakeholders

Multiple government bodies, committees, and industry participants are involved in getting a medicine listed on the PBS. Each plays a distinct role in the process.

## Key Stakeholders

| Stakeholder | Role | Key Output |
|-------------|------|------------|
| **TGA** | Regulatory approval (safety, quality, efficacy) | Marketing authorization |
| **PBAC** | Clinical and cost-effectiveness evaluation | Recommendation to Minister |
| **PBPA** | Price negotiation with sponsor | Agreed price and conditions |
| **MSAC** | Evaluates medical devices and procedures | Separate from PBS medicines |
| **Health Minister** | Final listing decision authority | Listing approval |
| **Sponsors** | Submit applications, provide evidence, negotiate price | Submissions and evidence |
| **PBS** | The scheme itself — vehicle for subsidized access | Published schedule |

## Detailed Roles

### TGA (Therapeutic Goods Administration)

The TGA is Australia's regulatory authority for therapeutic goods. Before a medicine can be considered for PBS listing, it must be registered on the **Australian Register of Therapeutic Goods (ARTG)**.

- Evaluates safety, quality, and efficacy
- Registration is a **prerequisite** for PBS listing
- Operates independently of the PBS listing process

### PBAC (Pharmaceutical Benefits Advisory Committee)

PBAC is the independent statutory committee that evaluates whether a medicine should be subsidized on the PBS. Established under **Section 101 of the National Health Act 1953**.

- Meets 3 times per year (March, July, November)
- Assesses clinical effectiveness, cost-effectiveness, and budget impact
- The Minister **cannot override** a negative PBAC recommendation
- Publishes all decisions as Public Summary Documents (PSDs)

See [PBAC Overview](/pbac/overview/) for detailed information on how PBAC operates.

### PBPA (Pharmaceutical Benefits Pricing Authority)

After PBAC recommends a medicine, the PBPA negotiates the price with the sponsor.

- Sets the ex-manufacturer price
- Negotiates risk-sharing arrangements and managed-entry schemes
- Ensures value for money within PBAC's recommended framework

### MSAC (Medical Services Advisory Committee)

MSAC evaluates medical devices, diagnostic tests, and procedures — not medicines. It is listed here because it is sometimes confused with PBAC.

<Aside type="note">
MSAC is **not part of the PBS medicines listing process**. It covers the Medicare Benefits Schedule (MBS) for services and procedures. The PBS API does not contain MSAC-related data.
</Aside>

### Health Minister

The Minister for Health makes the final decision on whether to list a medicine on the PBS.

- Can only approve medicines that PBAC has positively recommended
- In practice, almost always approves once pricing is agreed
- Has discretion over timing of listing

### Sponsors (Pharmaceutical Companies)

Sponsors are the pharmaceutical companies that hold TGA registration for a medicine and apply for PBS listing.

- Prepare and submit applications to PBAC
- Provide clinical trial data and economic models
- Negotiate pricing with PBPA
- Can resubmit after rejection with new evidence

<Aside type="tip">
**Agent tip:** Sponsor information is not directly available through the PBS API. The API focuses on the published schedule data — items, pricing, restrictions, and programs. Sponsor details are found in PBAC Public Summary Documents on [pbac.pbs.gov.au](https://pbac.pbs.gov.au).
</Aside>

## How Stakeholders Connect

```
Sponsor → TGA (registration)
    ↓
Sponsor → PBAC (submission + evaluation)
    ↓
PBPA ↔ Sponsor (price negotiation)
    ↓
Minister (final approval)
    ↓
PBS Schedule (published monthly)
```
