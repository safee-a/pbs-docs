# How PBS Listing Works

Getting a medicine listed on the PBS is a multi-stage process involving regulatory approval, clinical evaluation, price negotiation, and ministerial decision. The entire journey typically takes 6 to 17 months.

## The Listing Pipeline

```
TGA Registration
       ↓
PBAC Submission
       ↓
PBAC Evaluation (meets 3x/year)
       ↓
PBPA Price Negotiation
       ↓
Minister Approval
       ↓
PBS Schedule Listing (1st of month)
```

## Stage-by-Stage Breakdown

### 1. TGA Registration

Before a medicine can be considered for the PBS, the sponsor (pharmaceutical company) must obtain registration from the **Therapeutic Goods Administration (TGA)**. TGA evaluates safety, quality, and efficacy. This is a prerequisite — no TGA registration means no PBS listing.

### 2. PBAC Submission

Once TGA-approved, the sponsor submits an application to the **Pharmaceutical Benefits Advisory Committee (PBAC)**. The submission includes clinical trial data, economic modelling, and a proposed price. See [submission types](/pbac/submission-types/) for the different application categories.

### 3. PBAC Evaluation

PBAC meets **three times per year** (March, July, November) to evaluate submissions. The committee assesses clinical effectiveness, cost-effectiveness, and budget impact. See [evaluation criteria](/pbac/evaluation-criteria/) for details on how PBAC makes its decisions.

<Aside type="note">
The Health Minister **cannot override** a negative PBAC recommendation. If PBAC rejects a submission, the medicine cannot be listed on the PBS unless the sponsor resubmits with new evidence and receives a positive recommendation.
</Aside>

### 4. PBPA Price Negotiation

After a positive PBAC recommendation, the **Pharmaceutical Benefits Pricing Authority (PBPA)** negotiates the final price with the sponsor. This includes the ex-manufacturer price, any risk-sharing arrangements, and special pricing agreements.

### 5. Minister Approval

The Health Minister makes the **final listing decision** based on the PBAC recommendation and agreed pricing. In practice, the Minister almost always approves positively recommended medicines once pricing is agreed.

### 6. PBS Schedule Publication

The medicine appears in the next available **PBS schedule update**, published on the **1st of each month**. The listing includes the item's PBS code, pricing, restrictions, and program assignment.

<Aside type="tip">
**Agent tip:** Use the [`summary-of-changes`](/endpoints/summary-of-changes/) endpoint to detect newly listed medicines each month. Cross-reference with the [`items`](/endpoints/items/) endpoint to get full details of new listings.
</Aside>

## What Happens After Listing

Once listed, a medicine's PBS entry can change over time:

- **Price reductions** — mandatory price disclosure cycles or voluntary reductions
- **Restriction changes** — PBAC may recommend broadening or narrowing access criteria
- **New indications** — sponsor submits for additional clinical uses
- **Delisting** — sponsor withdraws, or medicine is removed for safety/commercial reasons

All changes appear in the monthly [schedule updates](/concepts/schedule-lifecycle/).

## Key Takeaways

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Prerequisite | TGA registration required before PBS submission |
| Decision maker | PBAC recommends; Minister decides |
| Veto power | Minister cannot list without positive PBAC recommendation |
| Update frequency | Monthly, on the 1st |
| Typical duration | 6-17 months from submission to listing |
