# Authority Prescribing

Some PBS items require **prior approval** before they can be prescribed at the subsidized price. This system ensures expensive or high-risk medicines are used appropriately and in line with clinical guidelines.

## Restriction Levels

PBS items fall into three levels of prescribing restriction, from least to most restrictive:

| Restriction Level | Prior Approval? | How to Prescribe |
|-------------------|----------------|-----------------|
| **Unrestricted** | No | Any approved prescriber can prescribe freely |
| **Restricted** | No (but must meet criteria) | Prescriber must satisfy clinical criteria; no approval step |
| **Authority Required** | Yes | Prescriber must obtain approval before or at time of prescribing |

### Unrestricted

The prescriber writes the prescription with no additional requirements. The medicine is fully subsidized for any indication.

### Restricted

The prescriber must ensure the patient meets the stated clinical criteria (e.g., "for the treatment of hypertension"), but does not need to seek external approval. The prescriber self-certifies that the criteria are met.

### Authority Required

The prescriber must obtain approval from Services Australia before prescribing. The method of approval varies by item.

## Authority Required Subdivisions

Authority Required items are further divided by how the prescriber obtains approval:

| Method | Description | Complexity |
|--------|-------------|-----------|
| **Streamlined** | No prior contact needed; prescriber writes a streamlined code on the script | Simplest |
| **Telephone/HPOS** | Prescriber contacts Services Australia via phone or the HPOS online portal | Moderate |
| **Written** | Prescriber submits a written application (rare, complex cases) | Most complex |

<Aside type="tip">
**Agent tip:** Streamlined authority items are the most common type of Authority Required listing. They require no phone call or application — just a 4-digit code on the prescription. See [Streamlined Authority](/prescribing/streamlined-authority/) for details on how to find these codes via the API.
</Aside>

## How Authority Approval Works

### Streamlined Authority
1. Prescriber confirms the patient meets clinical criteria
2. Prescriber writes the **streamlined authority code** on the prescription
3. No phone call or written application required
4. Pharmacist dispenses based on the code

### Telephone/HPOS Authority
1. Prescriber contacts Services Australia (phone: 1800 888 333, or via HPOS portal)
2. Provides patient details and clinical justification
3. Receives an **authority approval number**
4. Writes the approval number on the prescription

### Written Authority
1. Prescriber completes a written authority application form
2. Submits to Services Australia with clinical evidence
3. Waits for approval (can take days to weeks)
4. Receives written approval before prescribing

<Aside type="note">
Written authority is reserved for the most complex or expensive medicines. Most authority items use either streamlined or telephone approval.
</Aside>

## Querying Authority Requirements

Use the restriction hierarchy to determine what authority is needed for a specific item:

```bash
# Get restrictions for a PBS item
curl "https://data-api.health.gov.au/pbs/api/v3/restrictions?schedule_code=2026-02-01&pbs_code=12345A"

# Get parameters (condition groups) for a restriction
curl "https://data-api.health.gov.au/pbs/api/v3/parameters?schedule_code=2026-02-01&res_code=1234"

# Get criteria (specific requirements) for a parameter
curl "https://data-api.health.gov.au/pbs/api/v3/criteria?schedule_code=2026-02-01&parameter_id=5678"
```

The restriction response includes the `authority_method` field indicating which type of authority applies.
