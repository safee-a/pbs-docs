# Section 100 Programs

Section 100 of the *National Health Act 1953* enables alternative pharmaceutical supply arrangements outside the standard community pharmacy PBS model. These programs address situations where medicines require specialist oversight, hospital administration, or special access pathways.

## Overview

Standard PBS medicines are dispensed through community pharmacies. Section 100 programs provide alternative channels for medicines that:

- Are too complex or risky for standard dispensing
- Require hospital administration or monitoring
- Serve populations with access barriers
- Need special funding arrangements

## Key Programs

### Highly Specialised Drugs (HSD) Program

The largest Section 100 program, covering complex and expensive medicines for serious conditions.

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Program code** | `HB` |
| **Dispensing** | Approved hospital pharmacies only |
| **Prescribers** | Specialists at approved centres |
| **Examples** | Biologics, oncology agents, immunosuppressants, antivirals |
| **Patient cost** | Standard PBS copayments apply |

Patients must attend an approved hospital or outpatient clinic to collect their medicines. Community pharmacies cannot dispense HSD items.

### Efficient Funding of Chemotherapy (EFC)

Covers hospital-administered chemotherapy and related supportive medicines.

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Program code** | `CA` |
| **Dispensing** | Hospital pharmacies and day oncology units |
| **Prescribers** | Oncologists and haematologists |
| **Funding** | Direct to hospitals based on usage |

<Aside type="note">
EFC items are funded directly to hospitals rather than through the standard PBS claim process. The pricing and dispensing rules differ from community pharmacy items.
</Aside>

### Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander Health Services

Provides PBS access through Aboriginal Medical Services (AMS) and remote area health clinics.

- Addresses pharmaceutical access barriers in remote and very remote communities
- Medicines supplied directly through health service clinics
- Removes the need for patients to visit a community pharmacy
- Particularly important where the nearest pharmacy may be hundreds of kilometres away

### Growth Hormone Program

- Subsidized growth hormone for eligible patients (primarily children with growth hormone deficiency)
- Requires specialist authorization and ongoing monitoring
- Administered through approved hospital pharmacies and specialist clinics

### Opiate Dependence Treatment Program

- Covers methadone and buprenorphine (including buprenorphine/naloxone combination products)
- For treatment of opioid dependence
- Dispensed through approved pharmacies and clinics with specific dosing supervision requirements

## Querying Section 100 Programs via the API

### List All Programs

```bash
curl "https://data-api.health.gov.au/pbs/api/v3/programs?schedule_code=2026-02-01"
```

### Filter Items by Program

```bash
# Get all Highly Specialised Drugs
curl "https://data-api.health.gov.au/pbs/api/v3/items?schedule_code=2026-02-01&program_code=HB"
```

### Get Program-Level Dispensing Rules

Each program may have specific dispensing rules that apply to all items within it:

```bash
curl "https://data-api.health.gov.au/pbs/api/v3/program-dispensing-rules?schedule_code=2026-02-01&program_code=HB"
```

<Aside type="tip">
**Agent tip:** When checking dispensing rules for an item in a Section 100 program, query both the item-level [`dispensing-rules`](/endpoints/dispensing-rules/) endpoint and the [`program-dispensing-rules`](/endpoints/program-dispensing-rules/) endpoint. Program-level rules set defaults that item-level rules may override.
</Aside>
